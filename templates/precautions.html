Diabetic Retinopathy Prevention

Tips to protect your vision

1. Control Your Blood Sugar

Maintaining good blood sugar control is the most important factor in preventing diabetic retinopathy.

  • Monitor your blood glucose levels regularly
  • Follow your doctor's medication recommendations
  • Maintain a healthy diet low in processed sugars

2. Manage Blood Pressure

High blood pressure can accelerate the development of retinopathy.

  • Keep blood pressure below 130/80 mmHg
  • Reduce sodium intake
  • Exercise regularly

3. Maintain Healthy Cholesterol Levels

High cholesterol can contribute to retinal blood vessel damage.

  • Eat a diet low in saturated fats
  • Consider medication if lifestyle changes aren't enough

4. Get Regular Eye Exams

Early detection is key to preventing vision loss.

  • Type 1 diabetes: First exam within 5 years of diagnosis, then annually
  • Type 2 diabetes: First exam at diagnosis, then annually
  • More frequent exams if retinopathy is detected

5. Quit Smoking

Smoking increases the risk of diabetes complications, including retinopathy.

  • Seek help from smoking cessation programs if needed
  • Consider nicotine replacement therapy

6. Exercise Regularly

Physical activity helps control diabetes and its complications.

  • Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week
  • Include both aerobic and resistance training
